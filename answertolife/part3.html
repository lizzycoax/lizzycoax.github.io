Individual consciousness


Sentience

As humans go through their life, they gain more needs as they experience and think about desires.
This usually happens in childhood, when they have less resolve and stumble onto them.
Getting exposed to extreme fulfillment of desires gives them fetishes as well.
Since most humans have had different experiences and thoughts, their needs can differ in any situation.


Sapience

When a baby is taken out of the womb, it is removed from the safe environment of the womb.
They are then forced to understand the world around them to survive.


Self awareness

When the world is understood enough, humans start being aware about themselves to a progressively deeper degree.
This allows them to experience from an outside point of view, giving them more consciousness.
Since beliefs can be false, humans can experience self awareness about things that arent there.


Societal consciousness

Modes of thinking that groups of humans progress through.


1: Autistic

Survival - Naive
Fulfilling their own desires.

2: Sharing

Survival - Good
Fulfilling the desires of others for a reward.

3: Selfish

Survival - Bad
Getting others to fulfill their desires without giving back.
This is good for the individual but bad for the group.

4: Traditional

Morality - Naive
Labeling humans as good or bad based on whether they follow rules.
Since other groups have different rules, this can label individuals from those groups as bad.

5: Modern

Morality - Good
Respecting the rules of other groups when interacting with them.

6: Post modern

Morality - Bad
Allowing individuals to follow the rules of their group in other groups.
Since groups have a reason for their rules, this can lead to a dysfunctional society.

7: Sanctifying

Spirituality - Naive
Seeing that other humans act differently from them.

8: Holistic

Spirituality - Good
Seeing that humans act the way they do due to their experiences.

9: Mindful

Spirituality - Final
Holding no value to experience.


Degradation of consciousness

A process that degrades consciousness when consciousness is already low, such as when the human hasnt matured or is unaware of this process.


1: Trauma

An event outside of a humans control that causes pain or makes them cause harm, or makes them believe that will happen.
Such events include getting tortured, seeing other humans die and discovering they have harmful desires.

2: Repression

Avoiding anything that might cause a situation that traumatized them and not thinking about it.
Repression persists for as long as the human is unaware about what they arent thinking about.

3: Dissociation

When humans do get in a situation that traumatized them they have no control over themselves since they havent processed the situation.
If the situation they dissociate from is long term, such as abusive childhoods, seperated selves can develop.

4: Addiction

Repression can cause humans to not think about the desires that get them into harmful situations.
This along with dissociation further enforces their idea that they have no control over themselves, causing a cycle of addiction.

5: Depression

When humans repress enough of their desires they can become unable to see happiness.

6: Suicide

When depression gets strong enough, humans can see only pain in the future.
Since nothing is better than pain, killing themselves becomes a better alternative.


Autism & ADHD


1: Situational unawareness

Autists have less emotional intuition compared to others.

2: Adaption

Autists more often need to process emotions as opposed to having an intuition about them already.

3: Overstimulation

When too much information enters the brain that has not been adapted to, it can make humans not able to respond correctly.

4: Avoidance

Humans avoid situations in which they get overstimulated.
For autists this typically includes eye contact, confrontations and groups.

5: Lack of relation

Avoidance of being close to others can cause a lack of relation to them.

6: Unfit beliefs

Many beliefs that society has are based on humans being able to handle certain situations and relating to eachother.
When a human has those beliefs but does not have the qualities it is based on, it can make following its rules less fulfilling than believed.

7: Attention defecit

A task that is believed to be more fulfilling than it actually is can cause dissociation to occur, causing the task to be abandoned.

8: Hyperfixation

Tasks that fall outside of unfulfilling beliefs can seem much more fulfilling compared to other tasks.
